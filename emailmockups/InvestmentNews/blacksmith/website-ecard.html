Business
Blacksmith Brands markets, sells and distributes five over-the-counter (OTC) consumer products brands: EFFERDENT® denture cleaner; EFFERGRIP® denture adhesive cream; LUDEN'S® throat drops; NASALCROM® nasal allergy relief spray; and PEDIACARE® children's cough and cold remedies.

Investment      
In October 2009, Charlesbank partnered with an experienced management team led by Peter Mann, former Chairman and CEO of Prestige Brands, and Dana Schmaltz, former President of J.W. Childs, to acquire the five orphan brands from Johnson & Johnson (J&J). Management had deep knowledge of the OTC categories of the acquired brands and prior success in revitalizing orphan brands. Charlesbank recognized an opportunity to build on the brands’ extraordinary consumer awareness and to grow them through dedicated sales support, investment and innovation.

Our Role
Charlesbank worked closely with management to create a plan to reinstate advertising and promotion (A&P) activity, refresh product packaging, improve retail distribution and develop new products. During the four-month transition period from J&J, management built the infrastructure and developed the third-party relationships necessary to establish a stand-alone company and execute on the plan.

Outcome
The company’s aggressive sales efforts, investments in A&P and rapid new-product development drove greater-than-anticipated growth. The company launched 16 new Pediacare and Luden’s products, most of which achieved strong distribution at top retailers. Through the 12 months trailing October 2010, Blacksmith achieved almost 30% growth in revenue and 80% growth in EBITDA, exceeding our longer-term projections. Retailer and consumer demand for Blacksmith’s products confirmed our investment hypothesis that latent consumer equity in the brands could be unlocked by a dedicated management team and strategic investment.

About Charlesbank Capital Partners
Based in Boston and New York, Charlesbank Capital Partners is a middle-market private equity investment firm managing more than $2 billion of capital. Charlesbank focuses on management-led buyouts and growth capital financings, typically investing $50 million to $150 million per transaction in companies with enterprise values of $100 million to $750 million. The firm seeks to partner with strong management teams to build companies with sustainable competitive advantages and excellent prospects for growth.
