Earlier today, we posted to the data room (as files 1.2 and 2.2) new versions of the Investment Performance section of the Charlesbank PPM and page 15 of the Charlesbank Overview Presentation, each of which:

  1. corrects an error in the derivation of certain IRR numbers calculated to aggregate multiple funds (e.g., “inception to date IRR”), as they were inconsistent with the PPM definition of “net IRR”; and
  2. provides updated performance data for each of our funds as of March 31, 2014.

For your convenience, we have attached the data room postings, which include both the corrections and the updated performance information.

While we regret the need to revise the aggregated performance data, we want to assure you that the gross and net IRR information provided for each fund is not affected: the fund-level performance data you have received, in the PPM and the data room, remains accurate and unchanged, as does the net cash flow data presented in the data room.

By way of explanation, we discovered this issue as we updated the data room for our March results. We realized that the “whole fund” and “private market equivalent” synthetic aggregated return calculations, used principally to compare our returns to the S&P 500 returns, employed a methodology that we had developed when we included the pre-spinout Harvard funds in our track record, which required certain pro forma adjustments. This methodology should not have been carried forward, as (1) we no longer include the pre-1998 (Harvard) funds in our performance, and (2) the resulting presentation is not consistent with the definition of “net IRR” used throughout the balance of our materials.

Given the short time between now and our planned June 30th closing, we want to make sure that you are fully aware of the correction and the updated March 31 performance information, both of which we have summarized below:
